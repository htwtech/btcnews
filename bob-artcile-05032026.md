# Bitcoin Is Recovering. Your Yield Isn't. 

Holding Bitcoin through a downturn is one thing. 
Holding it through a recovery and still not doing anything with it - that's where it starts to feel like a missed opportunity.

We've been watching this cycle closely, and one thing keeps standing out: idle capital stays idle even when the asset itself is recovering. 

People hold, they wait, they watch the charts. Which is completely understandable, BTC holders are used to thinking long-term, and that instinct has served them well. 

But there's a reasonable question underneath all of that. Most BTC holders don't want to sell, don't want to wrap, don't want to trust a third party with custody! The problem has always been that those constraints left very little room to actually do anything with the asset. 

For most of Bitcoin's history that wasn't really possible without making uncomfortable tradeoffs. The infrastructure simply wasn't there. And honestly, that's still true for most of the ecosystem today, which is part of why only around 0.3% of all BTC ever touches DeFi. That gap is what BOB Gateway was built around. The mechanics are worth understanding: when you withdraw, you lock tokens in the Gateway contract on BOB, and an LP sends you BTC from their own funds immediately, so no waiting on a bridge. 
Gateway then verifies the Bitcoin transaction on-chain and releases the tokens to the LP as reimbursement. The LP carries the liquidity risk in exchange for the fee. Nothing is obscured, and the settlement is verifiable.

The interesting thing about bear markets is that they compress valuations but they don't compress time. Every month BTC sits idle is a month of potential yield that doesn't compound. At scale, and across the ~19 million BTC in circulation, so that's an enormous amount of capital doing nothing. 

The recovery changes the price. It doesn't change that math.
