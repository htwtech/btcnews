# The $1.3T problem Bitcoin hasn't solved yet

Bitcoin has $1.3T in market cap and 0.3% of it is actually deployed.
We look at this and see a structural problem, not a bear market issue.

The asset itself is fine. But the financial stack around it was never built. Every other major asset class has infrastructure that makes capital productive by default. Bitcoin just has cold storage and "hold."

That worked when BTC was a niche bet. It doesn't work when institutions are sitting on billions.

The demand is obvious. Institutions keep accumulating, but right now all that capital is sitting idle. They aren't scared of the risk, they just lack the tooling at scale. If a fund wants yield or wants to borrow against their bags, they are forced to use custodial wrappers and sketchy bridges. That introduces the exact counterparty risk they are trying to avoid.

### The missing layer 

This massive gap in the market is exactly why we keep building. Not just another network trying to farm volume - we are actively putting together the exact banking stack to solve this bottleneck.

Build on Bitcoin (BOB) Gateway lets you swap native BTC across 11 chains in a single tx without the usual bridging overhead. The next logical step is making that capital productive. You drop assets into Vaults, and the contracts automatically route them to generate yield so you don't have to manually ape into pools.

Everything runs on smart contracts, secured by both Ethereum ZK proofs and BTC finality. Lending and payments are next up.

The technical argument for Bitcoin-native finance is straightforward. The harder question is whether the infrastructure can mature fast enough to catch the institutional wave before it finds a different rail.

That part is still being answered.
